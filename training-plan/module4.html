What questions do you need to ask?

Questions

  • Time and place for questions to be asked
  • Know when to talk to a person and when to ask Google
  • Don't be afraid to reach out for help

No such thing as a dumb question, but there is a correct time and place for every question to be answered.

Objective questions are good for search engines, but more subjective/guidance questions should be reserved for team members or supervisors.

You shouldn't be afraid to reach out for help if you ever feel stuck, but there needs to be effort on your end too. There won't be any learning taking place if you don't take time to critically think about the problems first.

Questions

Good

  • Which of these tools/methods would you recommend and why?
  • Have you had any experience with this particular problem in the past?
  • Can you review my code so I can be sure I'm moving in the right direction?
  • I'm getting an error, and here is what I think it means...have you ever had any experience with it before?

Questions

Bad

  • Where should I start?
  • I'm getting an error, here's what it says...I don't know what to do.
  • I'm done, what should I do now?

Don't let fear of the unknown paralyze you, but don't waste people's time *(especially for objective questions).
